Internet Privacy

May 11, 2020 in Internet

Information is power. Information about you, means power over you… more or less.
What do you actually know about Internet privacy? How do you protect your online privacy?

This is what I have learned until now.

1. Things about Cookies

Cookies are files that contain information about your visit on a specific site. The information is saved on your local machine and then it is sent back to the website when you access it again. First-party cookies are used ‘internally’ to the site. However many sites will allow for third-party cookies which are created by other parties in order to provide information to advertising networks.
Third-party cookies can be blocked with ad-blockers. However, there are other little-tracking-monsters which are more difficult to avoid: like flash cookies and fingerprinting techniques. Google it if you want to find out more.

2. HTTP protocol

If you are interacting with a website which is using HTTP protocol… all the data that you are exchanging can be monitored (it is not encrypted in any way).
HTTPS encrypts information… so be sure that next time you check for that HTTPS before entering any personal information on a website.

3. Social media privacy

Maybe it’s time to check again the privacy settings on your LinkedIn, Facebook, Twitter, etc., accounts. Do you want your profile to be accessible to everyone or just to your actual and well-known connections?

4. End-to-end encryption for messaging apps

Even though your messages are encrypted when they are sent over the Internet, apps like Messenger and Telegram decrypt the messages upon receiving then and they store the decrypted information on the servers. |This is called encryption in transit. You will have to use the secret chat option in order to have those messages stored encrypted (end-to-end encryption).
WhatsApp uses end-to-end encryption and thus no one else can actually read your conversations, not even de the service provider.
